Lumpia

Description

These crispy Filipino egg rolls are filled with an aromatic pork and beef filling, and served with your choice of dipping sauce. Try them with banana ketchup, sweet chili sauce, or vinegar and soy dipping sauce.

Link to Original Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 (12 ounce) package lumpia wrappers
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • ⅓ cup finely chopped onion
  • ⅓ cup finely chopped green bell pepper
  • ⅓ cup finely chopped carrot
  • 1 quart oil for frying

Steps

  1. Thaw lumpia wrappers if frozen. Lay several wrappers on a clean, dry surface and cover with a damp towel to prevent wrappers from drying out.
  2. Mix beef, pork, onion, green pepper, and carrot together in a medium bowl. Place about 2 tablespoons of meat mixture along center of wrapper. Do not overstuff wrappers or they will burn before meat is cooked. Fold one edge of wrapper over filling. Fold outer edges in slightly, then roll into a closed cylinder. Wet finger with water and moisten outer edge to seal. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling, keeping finished lumpia covered to prevent drying.
  3. Heat oil in a 9-inch skillet on medium to medium-high heat until oil reaches 365 to 375 degrees F (170 to 175 degrees C). Fry 3 to 4 lumpia at a time, turning in oil until nicely browned, about 2 to 3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Cut each lumpia into thirds for parties, and serve with banana ketchup, if you like.
